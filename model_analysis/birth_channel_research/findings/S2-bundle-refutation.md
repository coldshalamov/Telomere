## What I attacked and what happened (the honest narrative)

### 1. The seam: the lane's count has ZERO inter-record constraint

`P2-bundle_survivor.count_survivors` (lines 457-478) creates `filled = set()`
**once** and **never mutates it**. So each bundle's surviving epochs are counted
in ISOLATION, and `S_epoch = prod_i base_i = base^R` is true *by construction*,
not by measurement. The findings then *assert* (unproven) that cross-bundle
fill "only ADDS pruning (lowers the base), never bends exponential to
polynomial." **That assertion was the only place the verdict could flip.** So I
built the joint DFS the lane skipped.

### 2. My round-1 "real channel" — and why it was WRONG

My first joint DFS handed the decoder the board's true `skip_slots` /
`literal_slots` / `seed_slots` as hard filters and required the chosen child
slots to exactly cover the observed holes. Result: `S_joint` collapsed toward
**O(1)** (T=1000, R=5: ISO=16.8 → JOINT=1.82). That *looks* like a real pin.

**It is an artifact.** A packed Telomere wire emits **nothing** at a hole
(`v1_roundtrip_proof` serialize: `if p in skip: continue`). The decoder
therefore **cannot observe** which board slots are holes. Hole positions ARE the
birth information — forensics Q1, verbatim: *"the decoder cannot know where the
children belong without unwinding the permutation, and unwinding requires k."*
By handing the DFS the true hole set, I fed it the answer; of course it
back-solved the epoch. The collapse `#epochsets == S_joint` is the signature of
that (a known hole + known seed slot pins k from the orbit).

### 3. The corrected, faithful decode — survivors grow as base^R, NOT a pin

Stripping the injected hole set and walking the board exactly as v1 try_decode
(holes GENERATED by committed epochs, `filled` built incrementally), the joint
survivor count does **not** pin to O(1). **Three complementary layers of
evidence — note they use DIFFERENT q and prove DIFFERENT halves:**

**(1) Analytic, proven-by-math (the load-bearing fact).** Per-bundle distinct
surviving epochs have mean `base = 1 + (T-1)·q_bundle`, with
`q_bundle = avalid(2,16)/2^16 = 100/65536 = 0.001526` (E = 9.356 bits). `base > 1
strictly for ALL T ≥ 2`, and `log2(base) → log2(T) − E` as T grows. The R
bundles are independent (distinct seeds → independent uniform wrong-salt
digests) so the count is multiplicative: `base^R`, exponential in R. The free
budget E shifts the knee `K = 2^E ≈ 655`; it never touches the slope.

**(2) Real v1 encoder + try_decode, proven-by-construction (the no-discount
worst case).** The real decoder applies **no** explosion check at candidate-gen
(it splits the expansion into B-bit chunks and defers to the final checksum), so
its effective `q ≈ 1` and survivors before the checksum (`sols`) explode:

| N  | T=2 | T=3 | T=4 | T=5 | T=6 |
|---:|----:|----:|----:|----:|----:|
| 13 | 8   | 30  | 48  | 275 | 27* |
| 16 | 2   | 45  | 97  | 441 | 129*|

`*` the T=6 dip is the documented **benign encoder ceiling** (forensics Q3:
matches exhaust, the wire becomes bit-identical to a lower-effective-T wire),
not a decoder pin. The trend up to the ceiling — 45 → 97 → 441 — proves
**geometry + checksum does not pin** the epoch. Exercises arity-2 AND arity-3,
closing the higher-arity branch.

**(3) Faithful S2 toy, measured (even WITH the q-discount, base stays > 1).**
Same grammar/shuffle as the lane, explosion check ALWAYS applied with the wire's
seed at every bundle cursor (the earlier draft of this toy gated the check on a
cursor lookup that SKIPPED it for non-seed cursors, inflating survivors ~10000×
— that quirk is FIXED; survivors dropped to the analytic base^R, exactly as they
should). At T=120, base = 1.182, and the distinct surviving **epoch-sets** (the
*channel*, not placement) track `base^R`:

| R | base^R (=1.182^R) | measured #epochsets |
|--:|------------------:|--------------------:|
| 2 | 1.40 | 1.20 |
| 4 | 1.95 | 2.13 |
| 6 | 2.72 | 2.60 |

`#epochsets` **grows** with R, tracking 1.18^R — a *pin* would be `#epochsets ≡ 1`
for all R. It is not. The count is small only because T=120 ≪ K=655: below the
knee, base ≈ 1 and the epoch is **near-free in expectation** (`true_ok = 100%`
throughout — the true reading is always among survivors). That below-knee
near-freedom IS prize (b): the quantified **max-free-reach K ≈ 655 for arity-2**
— a *bounded* finite reach, fully consistent with impossibility of the
*unbounded* channel, NOT a refutation.

So the lane's unproven assertion is now **proven the right way**: holes-derived
cross-fill (granting either q≈1 or the real q=0.0015) does **not** drive every
base_i → 1. The channel does not pin. **Impossibility holds, and is sharpened**
by exhibiting exactly which injected input manufactures the false pin (the hole
set, §2) and confirming the multiplicative base^R law with the explosion check
actually applied.

## The second half of the mandate: is there a variant that DOES pin to O(1)?

The only construction in the repo that could pin a bundle epoch to O(1) is the
**affine-stride gap fingerprint** (CLAUDE.md/BRIEF call bundles "solved by
affine-stride"): the inter-child gap is `≈ 5^(T-k+1) mod P`, so a discrete log
reads k. Two independent obstructions, neither free:

1. **Observability (the airtight one).** The partner child is a **hole** — the
   wire emits nothing there. So the gap is **unobservable**; computing it needs
   the partner's position, which needs k. Circular. This is the same wall as the
   holes argument and it is *structural*, not probabilistic. (Consistent with
   forensics' measured content-blindness: 321 distinct-content wires →
   byte-identical candidate sets; position carries no epoch info.)

2. **Board ≥ T (the secondary one, carriage).** Even granting observability, the
   gap fingerprint pins distinct epochs to distinct gaps ONLY if the orbit is
   non-periodic over `[1,T]`, i.e. `ord(5 mod P) ≥ T`, which forces board size
   `P ≥ T`. Then the per-record carriage is `log2 P ≥ log2 T` = the birth bill
   itself (measured: T=4096 → P=4127 → 12.01 bits vs log2 T = 12.00). The pin
   does not beat the bill; it **relocates** it from stored-checksum to
   **wrap/carriage** — exactly the PCTB tax the BRIEF already ruled a dead end
   (22× bloat at 64 passes). And the orbit IS periodic at the lane's scale
   (ρ = ord(5 mod P) = O(N): N=16 → ρ=16), so on a fixed board epochs k and k+ρ
   are geometrically identical — geometry pins nothing; only the probabilistic
   explosion check thins.

Higher arity / different alphabet only raise `E = -log2(q_bundle)` and thus the
free intercept (knee `K = 2^E`); they never change the slope `log2(T) - E`. The
real-code run already explodes at arity-3, confirming this.

## Counting gate (mandatory)

> Free + content-blind + unbounded bundle epoch channel → would random data
> net-compress without bound?

**Yes — so it is not such a channel, and the leak is named twice over:**

- **Derive-by-search route (sections A/B; the real-code run):** survivors are
  `~base^R`, base `= 1+(T-1)q > 1` strictly. Deterministically selecting the
  true reading out of `S` survivors needs a referee of `log2 S = R*log2(base)`
  bits — **stored-bits** (checksum width), dual to **compute** (the DFS forks
  the real decoder must enumerate, measured exploding ~2×/pass in forensics).
- **Store-the-holes route (the round-1 artifact, section C):** making holes
  available to "pin for free" costs `log2 C(N, #holes)` bits of arrangement —
  **wrap/carriage** (N=100, 12 holes → ~50 bits). This is the PCTB position tax.

Either way the birth bill reappears. The free structural budget E shifts the
intercept K; it never touches the slope `log2(T) - E`. No pigeonhole violation.
